1. You are walking to your first class, and you see a giant mob of people yelling “Save the squirrels!” and obstructing the vehicular flow of traffic. Your best friend beckons you from the crowd. You have 20 minutes before your first class. Do you join in?



2. You join an organization that does philanthropy. They decide to throw a concert and charge everyone $5. For the grand finale, they want to display a firework show. They ask for your advice. Do you guys do it?



3. You go to back to your dorm. Your roommate has decided that they absolutely hate it here, and created a flyer to let everyone know that. On the flyer, there is obscene language. She asks you to help her distribute the flyers. Do you turn her down?



4. After your first class, you see a few students yelling and running around, and you don’t know why. You could work on your Chem project, but you don't really want to do homework. Do you join them?



5. Your friend is so sick that she can’t get out of bed. She needs to turn in an essay today; her professor won’t take it late. She gives you her login to print out the paper and turn it in for her. Do you do it?



6. Being a broke college student, your best friend decided to make a raffle to raise money to pay for food. You're not so rich yourself. Do you participate?



7. Your friend gives you a code to a computer lab on campus, a professor sees you in there. He comes in, and he asks for your ID. Do you give him your ID?



8. Your roommate wants to throw a party in your dorm room this weekend. It is promised that there will be no drinking, no drugs, just loud music and tons of people. Do you agree?



9. In your computer science class, a classmate asks for help in causing the university’s system to crash. Do you report it?



10. Your roommate is still sick. She wants to get food from the convenience store downstairs, but she can’t get out of bed. She hands you her ID to use downstairs and asks you to grab chicken noodle soup. Do you do it?




Question 1 - The correct answer is NO:
Because you joined in on the giant mob of squirrel enthusiasts and began screaming about the rights of the squirrels, you unknowingly violated the code of conduct by obstructing traffic.

Question 2 - The correct answer is NO:
By lighting the fireworks, you broke a rule in the Code of Conduct stating that the possession or use of fireworks, incendiary devices, or dangerous explosives is prohibited.

Question 3 - The correct answer is YES:
Your roommate and you get caught distributing the flyers. You broke a rule in the Code of Conduct: “Origination or circulation of any advertising media containing (a) false information, (b) misleading information, (c) obscene language, (d) patently offensive material, (e) the promotion of alcohol consumption, or (f) illegal activities/behavior is prohibited.”

Question 4 - The correct answer is NO:
Because you started running and yelling with the students, you broke a Code of Conduct rule; you were displaying lewd and disorderly behavior.

Question 5 - The correct answer is NO:
By using her login to help turn in an essay, you broke a rule: “Use of another individual’s (a) identification; (b) network, email or other university based account; and/or (c) related passwords.”

Question 6 - The correct answer is NO:
By using the raffle to pay for food, you are breaking a rule in the Code of Conduct: You are forbidden to "play or sponsor an unlawful game of chance for money or for anything of value (e.g a raffle) (a) on University premises or (b) at a University or student organization sponsored activity or event."

Question 7 - The correct answer is YES:
By not giving your ID to the professor, you broke a rule in the Code of Conduct: “a student is required to produce his/her University ID card upon the request of an official or law enforcement officer”.

Question 8 - The correct answer is NO:
By throwing the party, you are disturbing the peace and making it so everyone around you cannot sleep. This violates the student Code of Conduct.

Question 9 - The correct answer is YES:
Your professor stopped the two of you just in time before you and your classmate crash the university system, but you broke a rule in the code of conduct.

Question 10 - The correct answer is NO:
When you went downstairs to the convenience store, the cashier notices that the picture on the ID is not a picture of you. You have broken a rule in the Code of Conduct: “Use of another individual’s (a) identification; (b) network, email or other university based account; and/or (c) related passwords is prohibited.”

You answered them all right!
